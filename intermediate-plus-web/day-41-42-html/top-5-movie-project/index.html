Movie Rankings

My top 5 movies of all time

The Tolkien Universe

I've bundled all these into one entry as otherwise, my top 5 would be full.

This first movie was my introduction to the world of Tolkien. I love all the other movies in the franchise, too, but this one stands out as my first memory. It also came out at a time when life was good, so brings back good memories.

The Lord of the Rings

  • The Fellowship of the Ring - 2001
  • The Two Towers - 2002
  • The Return of the King - 2003

Starring: Elijah Wood, Ian McKellen, Viggo Mortensen, Sean Astin, Billy Boyd, Dominic Minaghan, Christopher Lee, Liv Tyler, Orlando Bloom, John Rhys-Davies, Sean Bean, Hugo Weaving, and Andy Serkis.

Fun Fact: The voice of Treebeard was John Rhys-Davies, who also played Gimli.

The Hobbit

  • An Unexpected Journey - 2012
  • The Desolation of Smaug - 2013
  • The Battle of the Five Armies - 2014

Starring: Martin Freeman, Ian McKellen, Richard Armitage, Benedict Cumberbatch, Lee Pace, Evangeline Lilly, Orlando Bloom, Luke Evans, Cate Blanchett, Hugo Weaving, Christopher Lee, Ian Holm, and Andy Serkis

Fun Fact: Bennedict Cumberbach played both Smaug and Sauron.

The Matrix - 1999

The first Matrix movie is one that I got on VHS for christmas in my teens. I sat in my room and watched it on repeat all day - yes I spend a lot of time rewinding the cassette. The idea behind the world of The Matrix got me hooked. I watched this movie so many times that I knew the entire script, word for word. Another one that brings back great memories.

Gladiator - 2000

Starring: Russell Crowe, Joaquin Phoenix, Connie Nielsen, Oliver Reed, Richard Harris, Djimon Hounsou, Derek Jacobi, Ralf Möller, and Tommy Flanagan

Fun Fact: Oliver Reed (who played Proximo) died before filming was complete, so some of his scenes were finished using a body double and CGI face replacement, which was groundbreaking for its time.

This is not one that brings back good memories, but I do have a keen interest in the Romans and Ancient Greeks. The story is great, telling of the fall and rise of a persons life, the plight of ambition over all else, and the downfall of power. A classic good vs evil told in a new way.

I've also just recently watched the sequel, which I thought was great, too.

Hackers - 1995

Starring: Jonny Lee Miller, Angelina Jolie, Jesse Bradford, Matthew Lillard, Laurence Mason, Renoly Santiago, Fisher Stevens, Alberta Watson, and Wendell Pierce.

Fun Fact:Fun Fact: Angelina Jolie and Jonny Lee Miller met on this set and were married for a short time after filming this movie.

I saw this one at the cinema with my then best friend. Not everybody had a computer back then, but my dad did and he let me play on it. My use involved only playing minesweeper, Myst, and doing my homework - we even had one of those nasty belt driven printers.

The movie opend my eyes to the possiblities of the internet and computing and is the trigger that drove me into becoming a tech nerd.

I still watch this a few times each year.

Leon: The Professional - 1994

Starring: Jean Reno, Natalie Portman, Gary Oldman, and Danny Aiello.

Fun Fact: Gary Oldman improvised the famous "EVERYONE!!!" line, making it one of the most iconic and meme-worthy moments in film history.

This one is not in my top 5 because we share the same name; in fact, I can't put my finger on why I like this one so much. The story and action are great, and the cast is fantastic

Fun fact: the young girl in this movie is Natalie Portman, she was only 11 at the time.

Honorable Mention

Dune

Starring: Timothée Chalamet, Zendaya, Rebecca Ferguson, Oscar Isaac, Josh Brolin, Stellan Skarsgård, Dave Bautista, Javier Bardem, Charlotte Rampling, Jason Momoa, Stephen McKinley Henderson, Florence Pugh, Austin Butler, Léa Seydoux, and Christopher Walken.

Fun Fact: TFor Dune: Part Two, Austin Butler (who plays Feyd-Rautha) stayed in character even off-set, speaking in his eerie, villainous voice and keeping the bleached eyebrows, much like his method approach for Elvis (2022).

The modern Dune fanchise is a masterpiece so far. By the end, the collection as a whole could become my 2nd favourite frahchise behind The Lord of the Rings, and ahead of The Matrix.
